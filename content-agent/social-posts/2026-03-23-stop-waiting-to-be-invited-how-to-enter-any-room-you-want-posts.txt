The invitation isn't coming.

That's not cynicism — it's strategy.

Most rooms weren't designed with women like us in mind. And the talented women still waiting to be noticed, sponsored, or handed a seat? That wait has no end date.

The women who build real influence stopped waiting a long time ago.

Here's what actually moves the needle:

→ Make yourself useful to someone already inside the room — genuinely, not transactionally. Show up with an insight, an introduction, a perspective they don't have. People remember who added value before asking for anything.

→ If the room doesn't exist, build it. A dinner series. A curated community. A co-hosted event. You don't need a massive platform. You need a clear point of view and the initiative to act on it.

→ Show up before you feel ready. Most people in high-level rooms are figuring it out too. The difference between who's in and who's out is rarely capability — it's proximity and repetition.

Visibility is a strategy. Stop editing yourself down before you walk through the door.

The discomfort doesn't disappear. You just get better at walking through it.
