My AI Stack for Marketing, Sales, and Revenue

The tools I actually use every week to generate leads, write content, run outreach, and grow revenue. Organized by business outcome, not by category.

May 18, 2026 · 10 min read

Most "AI stack" lists are 60 logos on a slide. That isn't useful. The question I get from clients, friends, and family is simpler: what do you actually use, and how does it translate into revenue?

This is that answer. Everything here is a tool I touch most weeks, organized by the business outcome it drives. If you only adopt one section, start with the one tied to the metric you most need to move.

A note on the spirit of this list: I am not affiliated with any of these vendors, and I am not trying to sell you software. I am trying to show you the leverage, so you can pick the smallest possible stack that gets you the result.

How I Think About AI for Revenue

Before tools, three principles I keep returning to:

1. AI is leverage on the thing you already do well. It will not invent a strategy you do not have. It compounds the strategy you do.

2. Pick fewer tools, use them deeper. Two tools mastered will outperform ten tools sampled. Switching costs are real.

3. Tie every tool to a metric. If a tool is not making a number go up (pipeline, content velocity, reply rate, close rate, retention), it is a hobby.

With that out of the way, here is the stack.

1. Generating Leads

Outcome: new qualified conversations into the top of the funnel.

  • Apollo.io Building and enriching targeted prospect lists by title, industry, funding stage, and intent signals.
  • Clay Multi-source enrichment and AI research on each lead. Clay is where I turn a flat list into a personalized list.
  • Instantly / Lemlist Cold email sending at scale, with AI variants, deliverability monitoring, and inbox rotation.
  • Claude or ChatGPT Drafting and editing the actual cold email copy. I never let AI send raw output. I edit every line.
  • Perplexity Pre-call research on a prospect, their company, recent news, and likely pain points.
Strategy translation: the win here is not "more emails sent." It is higher reply rate at lower volume. AI lets you write a genuinely personalized opening line for 200 prospects in the time it used to take to write 20. That is the unlock.

2. Content and Brand

Outcome: consistent presence, organic reach, and a body of work that earns trust before the first sales call.

  • Claude Long-form writing: articles, guides, frameworks, white papers. Best for nuance and structured thinking.
  • ChatGPT Brainstorming, ideation, alternate angles, and quick rewrites.
  • Buffer Scheduling and queueing social content across LinkedIn, Instagram, and X, with AI assist on captions and timing.
  • Opus Clip Turning one long video into 10 short-form clips with captions and hooks.
  • Canva (Magic Studio) On-brand graphics, carousels, decks, and lightweight video.
  • Midjourney / Ideogram Higher-quality custom imagery when stock photography would feel generic.
  • Descript Editing podcasts and video by editing the transcript. Removes filler words automatically.
  • ElevenLabs Voiceovers, audio versions of articles, and multilingual variants.
Strategy translation: the goal is content velocity without losing your voice. Train one tool (I use Claude) on your voice and reference docs, then use it as the first draft engine. Edit aggressively. The compounding effect of publishing weekly for a year is far bigger than the cost of any single tool.

3. Search, SEO, and Being Found

Outcome: people find you when they are already looking for what you do, both in Google and in AI answers.

  • Perplexity Research, source gathering, and seeing how AI engines summarize a topic right now.
  • SurferSEO / Frase On-page SEO scoring, related entities, and brief generation.
  • Ahrefs or Semrush Keyword and backlink research. Both have AI features now, but the data is the point.
  • Claude or ChatGPT Outlining articles against a keyword brief and drafting meta titles, descriptions, and schema.
  • ChatGPT with browsing Spot-checking how your brand appears in AI answer engines.
Strategy translation: the new SEO question is not only "do I rank on Google" but also "am I cited by ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Google's AI Overviews." Structured, well-sourced, well-formatted content gets pulled into AI answers. That is where future leads will discover you.

4. Sales Acceleration and Deal Velocity

Outcome: more meetings, shorter cycles, higher close rates.

  • HubSpot (Breeze AI) CRM, pipeline, sequences, and AI summaries of every contact and deal.
  • Gong or Fathom Recording, transcribing, and analyzing every sales call. Patterns across deals are gold.
  • Otter Lightweight transcription for internal meetings and discovery calls.
  • Claude Post-call follow-up emails, proposal drafts, and objection responses tailored to what was actually said on the call.
  • Calendly With AI routing, removing friction from meeting booking.
  • LinkedIn Sales Navigator Account-based prospecting and warm intro paths.
Strategy translation: the highest leverage move in sales is better follow-up. AI gives you the discipline you already wished you had. A 12-minute call becomes a transcript, a summary, a follow-up email, a CRM update, and a draft proposal in under five minutes.

5. Operational Leverage and Automation

Outcome: the team behaves like it is bigger than it is.

  • Zapier Connecting tools that do not natively talk to each other. The duct tape of any modern stack.
  • Make More complex automations with branching logic. Better for visual thinkers.
  • n8n Open-source workflow automation for anything sensitive that I want to self-host.
  • Claude Code My most-used tool. I build internal scripts, agents, and automations in natural language.
  • Cursor When I want a code editor with an AI pair. Faster iteration than chatting back and forth.
  • Notion AI Internal knowledge base with search and summarization across docs.
Strategy translation: automation is not about removing humans. It is about removing the boring 30 percent of every job so humans can spend more time on the work that compounds. Start by listing every weekly recurring task that takes more than 15 minutes. That list is your automation backlog.

6. Measurement and Attribution

Outcome: knowing what is working before you spend more on it.

  • GA4 Web analytics. Pair with BigQuery for custom queries that GA4's UI cannot answer.
  • Mixpanel or Amplitude Product analytics if you have an app or SaaS. Cohort and funnel analysis.
  • Common Room Tracking community and signal-based engagement across Slack, Discord, GitHub, and social.
  • Claude or ChatGPT Pasting in raw analytics exports and asking for the story. Surprisingly good at spotting anomalies.
Strategy translation: AI does not replace your analyst. It lowers the cost of asking a question by 95 percent. Ask more questions of your data than you used to. Most teams under-query their own analytics because the cost of asking used to be too high.

7. Strategy, Positioning, and Thinking Partner

Outcome: better decisions, made faster, with more perspectives in the room.

  • Claude (long context) Pasting in a full strategy doc, a deck, a contract, or a board pre-read and asking for honest critique.
  • ChatGPT Quick reframes, generating counter-arguments, and stress-testing positioning.
  • Gemini A second opinion on the same question. Different model, different biases, useful triangulation.
  • Lex or Sudowrite Long-form thinking and writing where I want a different feel.
Strategy translation: the best use of AI for a founder or operator is as a thinking partner who never gets tired and is not impressed by you. Ask it to argue against your plan. Ask it for the three things you are missing. The honesty cost is much lower than asking your team.

What This Means for You

Three suggestions, depending on where you are:

If you are a founder or solo operator, pick two tools from sections 1 and 2 and run them for 90 days. Lead generation and content. That is the engine.

If you are a marketing or revenue leader, the highest-leverage investment this year is probably section 5. Build an automation backlog and start checking off the boring 30 percent of every job on your team.

If you are AI-curious and trying to learn, start in section 7. Use Claude or ChatGPT as a daily thinking partner. The reps build intuition faster than any course.

The honest truth is that the tools matter less than the discipline of using them. The teams winning with AI right now are not the ones with the most logos on the slide. They are the ones who picked a few, used them every day, and measured the result.
