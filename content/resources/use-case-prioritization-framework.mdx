# GenAI Use Case Prioritization Framework

**For:** Strategy Teams, Innovation Leaders, Department Heads, CxOs
**Format:** Downloadable PDF + Article

---

One of the most common mistakes organizations make with AI is trying to do everything at once — or, equally, trying to find the "perfect" use case before starting anything.

The reality: most organizations have dozens of potential GenAI use cases. The competitive advantage goes to organizations that identify the right ones to pursue first and build the discipline to learn from them.

This framework gives you a structured, repeatable process to identify, score, and prioritize your GenAI use cases — so you can move from ideation to action with confidence.

---

## Why Prioritization Matters

Without a prioritization framework, organizations typically fall into one of three traps:

**The shiny object trap:** Chasing the most exciting use case rather than the most impactful one. Often involves expensive custom AI development for edge cases while high-volume, high-value use cases go unaddressed.

**The analysis paralysis trap:** Waiting for perfect information before starting. A 70% solution launched is worth more than a 100% solution still in committee.

**The bottom-up trap:** Letting individual teams choose their own AI projects without coordination. Results in redundant tooling, inconsistent governance, and missed organizational-scale opportunities.

A good prioritization framework gets you out of all three traps.

## The Framework: Three Steps

### Step 1: Generate Your Use Case Longlist

Start broad. Get the right people in a room — operations, IT, HR, finance, customer-facing functions, and leadership — and generate a list of 15–30 potential use cases. Use these prompt questions to get the conversation started:

- Where does your team spend time on repetitive, high-volume tasks?
- Where is quality inconsistent because it depends on individual judgment?
- What information do people frequently search for or compile manually?
- Where are there bottlenecks in handoffs between teams or systems?
- What customer or employee experience problems are recurring?

Don't filter at this stage. More ideas is better.

---

### Step 2: Score Each Use Case Against 6 Criteria

Rate each use case on a 1–4 scale across six dimensions:

| Criterion | What to Assess | 1 = Low | 4 = High |
|-----------|---------------|---------|---------|
| **ROI Potential** | Estimated time saved, revenue impact, or cost reduction | Minimal savings | Significant, measurable impact |
| **Data Availability** | Is the data needed accessible, clean, and usable? | No relevant data | Data is ready and accessible |
| **Risk Level** | Regulatory, reputational, and operational risk | High risk | Low risk |
| **Speed to Value** | How quickly can we get to measurable results? | 12+ months | Under 3 months |
| **Team Readiness** | Does the team have the skills and capacity to execute? | Major gaps | Team is ready |
| **Vendor Dependency** | Can this be done with existing tools, or does it require new vendors? | Requires major new infrastructure | Can be done with current tools |

**Scoring tip:** For your first wave of AI initiatives, weight Speed to Value and Data Availability heavily. Early wins build organizational confidence and momentum.

---

### Step 3: Map to the 2x2 Prioritization Matrix

Plot each use case on a 2x2 matrix:

```
HIGH IMPACT
      │ Quick Wins          │ Strategic Bets      │
      │ (Pursue First)      │ (Plan & Invest)     │
      ├─────────────────────┼─────────────────────┤
      │ Easy But Low Value  │ Hard & Low Value    │
      │ (Low Priority)      │ (Deprioritize)      │
LOW   └─────────────────────┴─────────────────────┘
                LOW EFFORT              HIGH EFFORT
```

**Quick Wins** (High Impact + Low Effort): Your first wave. These build momentum, demonstrate ROI, and create organizational proof points.

**Strategic Bets** (High Impact + High Effort): Your roadmap. These are worth the investment but need proper planning, resources, and governance.

**Low Priority** (Low Impact + Low Effort): Fine to pursue if resources are available, but don't prioritize them over strategic bets.

**Deprioritize** (Low Impact + High Effort): Say no. Politely but firmly.
