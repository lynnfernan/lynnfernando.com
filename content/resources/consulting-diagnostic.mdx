# What Kind of Consultant Are You?

Most consultants undersell themselves — not because they lack experience, but because they haven't named what they actually do.

You've spent years solving real problems at real companies. But when someone asks "what do you consult on?", the answer comes out vague. Too broad. Too humble. Or just not the thing that lights you up.

This diagnostic is designed to fix that. Three questions. Four archetypes. And a clear picture of where your consulting edge actually lives.

## The Four Archetypes

**The Brand Architect** makes companies culturally relevant and impossible to ignore. They work on positioning, narrative, identity, and the creative systems that make brands mean something.

**The Revenue Engineer** builds the commercial infrastructure that makes money move. Partnerships, channels, distribution, business development — they design the engine, not just the strategy.

**The Market Launcher** takes things from zero to real. Go-to-market, commercialization, early-stage growth — they know how to build something that didn't exist before.

**The Growth Transformer** changes how leadership teams see and pursue opportunity. They diagnose what's actually broken, architect the fix, and advise at the level of vision and direction.

## Who This Is For

- Executives transitioning into consulting or advisory work
- Independent consultants refining their focus and positioning
- Founders trying to package their expertise as a scalable offer
- Anyone who's good at a lot of things and needs to get specific

## How It Works

Answer three questions honestly — not about who you think you should be, but about where you've actually done your best work and where you want to spend your time.

Your result will show you your archetype, a description of your edge, and the specific offers you should be selling. Take the diagnostic on the right to get started.
