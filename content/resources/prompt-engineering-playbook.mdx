## What's Inside the Playbook

### The RCOF Prompt Framework

Every strong business prompt has four components:

| Component | What It Does | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **R**ole | Sets the AI's perspective and expertise | "You are a senior HR business partner..." |
| **C**ontext | Provides the background and situation | "...reviewing a performance improvement plan for a mid-level manager..." |
| **O**utput | Specifies what you want | "...and I need a structured 30-day performance improvement plan..." |
| **F**ormat | Defines how it should be structured | "...formatted as a numbered list with weekly milestones." |

Without all four components, you get generic output. With them, you get work-quality output.

### Sample Prompts by Function

#### Human Resources

**Writing a job description**
> "You are a senior HR leader at a mid-size technology company. I need to hire a Senior Product Manager for our SaaS platform. Write a compelling job description that covers the role summary, key responsibilities (5-7 bullets), requirements (must-have and nice-to-have), and a brief section on why someone would want this role. Format it for LinkedIn posting."

**Handling a difficult performance conversation**
> "You are an executive coach specializing in people management. I need to prepare for a difficult performance conversation with an employee who has been missing deadlines consistently. Give me: (1) the key points to cover, (2) language to use that is direct but not defensive, and (3) 3 questions to ask to understand the root cause. Format as a structured meeting guide."

**Summarizing employee feedback**
> "You are an HR analyst. I'm going to paste in 15 pieces of employee feedback from an engagement survey. Your job is to: (1) identify the top 3 themes, (2) note any urgent concerns, and (3) suggest 2-3 actionable responses. Format as an executive summary under 300 words. Here is the feedback: [PASTE FEEDBACK]"

#### Marketing

**Writing a content brief**
> "You are a content strategist at a B2B SaaS company. I need a content brief for a long-form article targeting mid-market CFOs. The topic is: 'How AI is changing financial forecasting.' Include: target audience and pain points, primary keyword, article structure with H2 headings, key points for each section, and suggested CTAs. Format as a content brief document."

**Repurposing content**
> "You are a social media manager. I'm going to paste in a 1,500-word blog post. Transform it into: (1) 3 LinkedIn posts under 200 words each with different angles, (2) 5 Twitter/X posts under 280 characters, and (3) a 3-email nurture sequence. Here is the article: [PASTE ARTICLE]"

**Writing a cold outreach email**
> "You are a senior business development professional. Write a cold outreach email to the Head of Operations at a regional healthcare system. The email should: reference a specific challenge in healthcare operations, introduce our AI workflow automation platform, and request a 20-minute call. Keep it under 150 words. Be specific, not generic. Do not use the phrase 'I hope this email finds you well.'"

#### Operations & Productivity

**Drafting a project update email**
> "You are a senior project manager. Draft a project status update email for executive stakeholders. The project is a CRM migration. Status: 60% complete, on schedule, one vendor delay on data mapping. Include: current status, what was completed this week, blockers, next steps, and a risk flag on the vendor delay. Format as a professional email, under 250 words."

**Creating a meeting agenda**
> "You are a chief of staff. Create a 60-minute strategic planning meeting agenda for a leadership team of 8. The goal is to align on Q3 priorities and resource allocation. Include: pre-meeting prep items, timed agenda sections, discussion questions for each section, and a clear decision output expected from the meeting. Format as a shareable document."

**Analyzing a vendor contract**
> "You are a contracts analyst. I'm going to paste in a vendor software agreement. Identify: (1) key obligations on our side, (2) any auto-renewal clauses and notice periods, (3) data privacy and ownership provisions, and (4) any unusual or risky terms. Format as a structured summary with a risk flag section. Here is the contract: [PASTE CONTRACT]"

---

#### Finance

**Writing a budget narrative**
> "You are a finance director at a non-profit. Write a budget narrative for a $250,000 technology modernization grant. The budget includes: $120K software licenses, $80K implementation services, $30K training, $20K contingency. Explain each line item with justification, link costs to program outcomes, and write in a tone appropriate for a foundation grant application."

**Summarizing a financial report**
> "You are a financial analyst. I'm going to paste in a quarterly earnings report. Provide: (1) a 3-paragraph executive summary, (2) the top 3 positive highlights, (3) the top 3 concerns or risks, and (4) a one-sentence overall assessment. Format for a board presentation. Here is the report: [PASTE REPORT]"

## The Before & After Difference

| Bad Prompt | Better Prompt (RCOF) |
|------------|---------------------|
| "Write me a performance review" | "You are an experienced manager. Write a mid-year performance review for a software engineer who has exceeded technical goals but needs improvement on communication. Include specific examples based on these notes: [NOTES]. Format as a formal review document with sections for achievements, areas for development, and goals." |
| "Summarize this" | "You are an executive assistant. Summarize the following board meeting transcript in under 200 words for the CEO. Highlight: key decisions made, open items requiring follow-up, and any risks mentioned. [TRANSCRIPT]" |
| "Write a LinkedIn post" | "You are a thought leadership writer. Write a LinkedIn post for the CEO of an AI company sharing 3 counterintuitive lessons from launching their first enterprise AI product. Use a conversational tone, avoid jargon, and end with a question to drive engagement. Under 250 words." |
