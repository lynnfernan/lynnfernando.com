## Keynotes

### 1. AI Is Already Here: What Leaders Need to Do Now
**Best for:** Executive conferences, industry associations, leadership summits
**Duration:** 30–60 minutes

The AI window isn't opening — it's already open. This keynote cuts through the noise and gives leaders a clear-eyed view of what Generative AI can and can't do, where the real business value lies, and the 3 decisions every leader needs to make in the next 90 days.

**Audience outcomes:**
- Understand what GenAI actually is — in plain language
- Identify the highest-leverage opportunities for their organization
- Know the governance and risk questions they need to be asking
- Leave with a clear next step, not just inspiration

---

### 2. The Human Side of AI: Leading Through the Transition
**Best for:** Company all-hands, leadership conferences, HR/people leader events
**Duration:** 30–60 minutes

The biggest risk in AI adoption isn't the technology — it's the people. This keynote addresses the fears, the change fatigue, and the real workforce questions your employees are asking (and not asking) about AI. It offers a framework for leading with both honesty and optimism.

**Audience outcomes:**
- Language and frameworks for honest, productive AI conversations
- How to communicate AI strategy in a way that builds trust
- How to invest in your people's AI readiness — not just your technology
- What "human + AI" looks like in practice

---

### 3. Practical GenAI: From Pilot to Organizational Capability
**Best for:** Technology conferences, innovation summits, ops and IT leader events
**Duration:** 45–60 minutes

Most organizations have run an AI pilot. Far fewer have turned it into lasting organizational capability. This keynote covers the common failure modes of AI adoption and the specific practices that distinguish organizations that scale AI from those that stall.

**Audience outcomes:**
- The 5 most common reasons AI pilots fail to scale
- What a mature AI operating model looks like
- How to build internal AI capability without hiring a data science team
- Practical governance frameworks that enable rather than block

## Workshops

### 4. GenAI Strategy Sprint (Half-Day)
**Best for:** Leadership teams, executive offsites, innovation retreats
**Duration:** 3.5 hours (half-day)
**Participants:** 8–20 leaders

A structured, facilitated workshop that takes your leadership team from awareness to a prioritized GenAI action plan — in a single session.

**Workshop structure:**
1. **Context setting** (30 min): Current landscape, what's real vs. hype, your industry context
2. **Opportunity mapping** (60 min): Structured use case ideation and scoring
3. **Readiness assessment** (45 min): Where you are across data, talent, governance, and use cases
4. **Roadmap building** (60 min): Priority use cases, owners, and 90-day next steps
5. **Governance foundations** (30 min): What policies and guardrails you need now

**Deliverable:** A 90-day GenAI action plan with prioritized use cases, owners, and governance commitments.

---

### 5. Prompt Engineering for Your Team (Half-Day or Full-Day)
**Best for:** Business teams, department-level workshops, L&D programs
**Duration:** 3.5 hours (half-day) or 7 hours (full-day)
**Participants:** 10–50 participants

A hands-on, function-specific workshop where participants learn to use Generative AI tools effectively for their actual work — with prompts and exercises tailored to your team's role.

**Workshop structure:**
- The RCOF framework for effective prompting
- Live practice with real work scenarios from your team
- Function-specific prompt libraries (HR, Finance, Marketing, Operations)
- Quality review: how to evaluate and improve AI outputs
- Policy and safe usage guidelines for your organization

**Deliverable:** Each participant leaves with a personal prompt library for their role and the confidence to use it immediately.

---

### 6. AI Governance & Risk for Non-Technical Leaders (2–3 Hours)
**Best for:** Legal, compliance, HR, and risk teams; board members; senior leadership
**Duration:** 2–3 hours
**Participants:** 8–25 participants

A practical, scenario-based session on the real risks of Generative AI — and how to manage them without blocking innovation. Participants work through real governance challenges and leave with a draft AI policy framework for their organization.

**Topics covered:**
- The AI risk landscape: hallucinations, bias, IP, data privacy, regulatory
- What a responsible AI use policy covers (and doesn't)
- How to evaluate AI tools for compliance and risk
- Board-level oversight of AI: roles, responsibilities, red lines
- Building a governance framework that enables AI, not just constrains it

**Deliverable:** Draft AI use policy and governance checklist for your organization.
