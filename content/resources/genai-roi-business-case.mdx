Whether you're pitching an AI pilot to your CFO, requesting budget from a board, or writing a proposal for leadership sign-off, you need a credible, structured business case that speaks the language of ROI, risk, and strategic alignment.

## Why Most AI Business Cases Fail

The most common reasons AI investment proposals get rejected or stalled:

**1. Vague value claims.** "This will make our team more efficient" is not a business case. Quantify it.

**2. Missing risk assessment.** Finance and legal teams will ask about risks. Having answers ready signals maturity.

**3. No clear success definition.** If you can't define what success looks like, don't expect budget.

**4. Underestimating change management.** The technology cost is often smaller than the people and process cost. Budget for both.

**5. Ignoring alternatives.** A credible business case considers the status quo and alternatives, not just the proposed solution.

## The Business Case Template

### Section 1: Executive Summary (1 page)

Provide a 3–5 sentence overview covering:

- **Problem:** What business problem or opportunity does this address?
- **Proposed Solution:** What AI capability or tool are you proposing?
- **Expected Outcome:** What are the key outcomes in business terms (time saved, revenue, cost reduction)?
- **Investment Required:** Total estimated cost (year 1 and ongoing)
- **Recommendation:** Approve / Approve with conditions / More information needed

---

### Section 2: Problem Definition

**2.1 Current State Description**
Describe the process, workflow, or challenge you're addressing. Be specific about:
- Who is affected (teams, roles, number of people)
- How frequently the problem occurs
- What the current workaround or solution is

**2.2 Cost of the Status Quo**
Quantify the current state cost. Use this formula as a starting point:

```
Annual cost of current process =
  (Average time per task in hours)
  × (Number of tasks per year)
  × (Fully-loaded hourly cost of the employee)
```

**Example:**
- Writing a weekly status report: 2 hours × 50 times/year × $75/hr = **$7,500/person/year**
- Across a team of 20: **$150,000/year**

**2.3 Strategic Alignment**
Connect the problem to 1–2 of your organization's strategic priorities. AI proposals that align with stated strategy are significantly more likely to get funded.

---

### Section 3: Proposed Solution

**3.1 Solution Description**
Describe the proposed AI capability in plain language. Include:
- What the AI does (not the underlying technology — the business function)
- How it integrates with existing workflows
- What the user experience looks like

**3.2 Vendor / Tool Options Considered**
List 2–3 options evaluated with brief rationale for your recommendation.

| Option | Pros | Cons | Annual Cost |
|--------|------|------|------------|
| Option A (Recommended) | ... | ... | $ |
| Option B | ... | ... | $ |
| Status Quo | ... | ... | (ongoing cost) |

**3.3 Implementation Approach**
Outline the implementation plan at a high level:
- Phase 1 (Pilot): Timeline, scope, success criteria
- Phase 2 (Rollout): Timeline, teams, change management
- Phase 3 (Optimize): Measurement, iteration

### Section 4: Financial Analysis

**4.1 Total Cost of Investment (TCI)**

| Cost Category | One-Time | Annual Recurring |
|--------------|---------|-----------------|
| Software / licensing | $ | $ |
| Implementation / setup | $ | — |
| Integration / IT work | $ | $ |
| Training | $ | $ |
| Change management | $ | — |
| Ongoing maintenance | — | $ |
| **Total** | **$** | **$** |

**4.2 Projected Benefits**

| Benefit Category | Calculation | Annual Value |
|-----------------|------------|-------------|
| Time savings (productivity) | Hours saved × hourly rate × FTEs | $ |
| Error reduction | Error rate × cost per error × volume | $ |
| Throughput increase | Additional output × value per unit | $ |
| Headcount avoidance | FTEs not hired × fully-loaded cost | $ |
| Revenue uplift | Conversion rate improvement × pipeline | $ |
| **Total Annual Benefit** | | **$** |

**4.3 ROI Calculation**

```
Year 1 Net Benefit = Total Benefits - (One-Time Costs + Year 1 Recurring Costs)
Payback Period = Total Investment ÷ Annual Benefit
3-Year ROI = ((3-Year Total Benefit - 3-Year Total Cost) ÷ 3-Year Total Cost) × 100
```

**4.4 Risk-Adjusted ROI**
Apply a confidence factor to your benefits projection based on your confidence in the estimates:
- High confidence (proven vendor, clear use case): apply 80–90% of projected benefits
- Moderate confidence (new use case, estimated data): apply 60–70% of projected benefits
- Low confidence (speculative): apply 40–50% of projected benefits

Present both optimistic and risk-adjusted scenarios.

### Section 7: Stakeholder Alignment Checklist

Before finalizing your business case, confirm alignment with the following stakeholders:

- [ ] **CFO / Finance:** Reviewed financial assumptions; cost and benefit estimates validated
- [ ] **IT / Security:** Technical feasibility confirmed; security review completed
- [ ] **Legal / Compliance:** Regulatory and contractual review completed
- [ ] **HR / People:** Change management and training plan reviewed
- [ ] **End Users:** At least one pilot group identified and consulted
- [ ] **Executive Sponsor:** Business priority and strategic alignment confirmed
