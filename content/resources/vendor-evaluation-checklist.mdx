# AI Vendor Evaluation Checklist

**For:** Procurement, IT Leaders, Business Decision-Makers
**Format:** Free Download — No Email Required

---

The GenAI vendor market is moving faster than any procurement team can track. New tools launch weekly. Vendors make bold claims. And the decisions you make today — which platforms you contract with, which vendors get access to your data, which tools your teams standardize on — will shape your AI capabilities for years.

This checklist gives you 40 rigorous questions across 7 evaluation categories to ask any GenAI vendor before signing a contract or rolling out a tool. Use it for enterprise platform evaluation, department-level tool selection, or quick vetting of point solutions.

---

## How to Use This Checklist

For each vendor, score each item:
- ✅ **Satisfied** — Vendor clearly meets this criterion
- ⚠️ **Needs Clarification** — Needs more information or contractual commitment
- ❌ **Not Met** — Vendor fails to meet this criterion (flag as risk)

Any ❌ in the Security/Compliance or Data Privacy categories should be treated as a potential dealbreaker.

---

## Category 1: Security & Compliance (8 Questions)

- [ ] Does the vendor maintain SOC 2 Type II certification? (Request the latest report)
- [ ] Is the vendor ISO 27001 certified or in progress?
- [ ] Is data encrypted at rest and in transit using industry-standard methods (AES-256, TLS 1.2+)?
- [ ] Does the vendor support single sign-on (SSO) and multi-factor authentication (MFA)?
- [ ] Can the vendor provide a completed security questionnaire (e.g., CAIQ/SIG)?
- [ ] What is the vendor's process for security incident notification? What are the SLAs?
- [ ] Does the vendor conduct regular penetration testing? Are results available on request?
- [ ] Is there a formal vulnerability management and patch management program?

**🚩 Red Flag:** Vendor is unable to provide a SOC 2 report or security questionnaire on request.

---

## Category 2: Data Privacy & Ownership (7 Questions)

- [ ] Does the vendor use your input data to train their models? (Must be explicitly stated in the contract — not just the ToS)
- [ ] Who owns the outputs generated by the AI? Is this clearly contractually defined?
- [ ] Where is data stored? Which countries/regions? Does this comply with your data residency requirements?
- [ ] Does the vendor support GDPR, CCPA, or other applicable privacy regulations? Can they provide a Data Processing Agreement (DPA)?
- [ ] What is the vendor's data retention policy? Can you request deletion of your data?
- [ ] Does the vendor have a process for handling data subject access requests (DSARs)?
- [ ] Is there an option for private deployment or enterprise-grade data isolation?

**🚩 Red Flag:** Vendor trains on your inputs by default with no opt-out, or cannot produce a DPA.

---

## Category 3: Model Quality & Reliability (6 Questions)

- [ ] What underlying AI models does the product use? Are they proprietary or third-party (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google)?
- [ ] How does the vendor handle hallucinations and factual accuracy? What guardrails are in place?
- [ ] Is there model versioning? Can you lock to a specific model version for consistency?
- [ ] What are the uptime SLAs? What is the vendor's historical uptime?
- [ ] Is there a test environment or sandbox for evaluation before production rollout?
- [ ] How frequently is the model updated, and how are changes communicated?

**🚩 Red Flag:** No uptime SLAs, no changelog for model updates, or vendor cannot describe hallucination mitigation.

---

## Category 4: Integration & Technical Fit (6 Questions)

- [ ] Does the product offer a well-documented REST API?
- [ ] Are there native integrations with your existing tools (CRM, ERP, HRIS, productivity suite)?
- [ ] What authentication and authorization methods are supported for API access?
- [ ] What are the rate limits and how do they scale with usage?
- [ ] Is there a software development kit (SDK) for your primary programming languages?
- [ ] Can the vendor provide architecture diagrams showing how data flows through their system?

---

## Category 5: Pricing & Commercial Terms (5 Questions)

- [ ] Is pricing based on usage (tokens/API calls), seats, or a flat fee? Does it scale predictably?
- [ ] Are there overage charges? What happens if you exceed your plan limits?
- [ ] Is there a minimum commitment period? What are the exit terms?
- [ ] Are there data portability provisions — can you export your configurations, fine-tunes, or data?
- [ ] What is the pricing for additional users, storage, or compute as your usage grows?

**🚩 Red Flag:** Pricing is opaque, highly variable, or the vendor cannot provide a cost estimate for your use case.

---

## Category 6: Support & Customer Success (4 Questions)

- [ ] What support tiers are available, and what is the response time SLA for critical issues?
- [ ] Is there dedicated customer success or implementation support included?
- [ ] Does the vendor provide training resources, documentation, and onboarding assistance?
- [ ] Is there a community, user group, or customer advisory board?

---

## Category 7: Vendor Stability & Strategic Fit (4 Questions)

- [ ] How long has the vendor been operating? Are they well-funded or revenue-generating?
- [ ] What is the vendor's product roadmap for the next 12 months? Does it align with your needs?
- [ ] Can the vendor provide reference customers similar to your organization (industry, size)?
- [ ] Is the vendor willing to negotiate a Master Service Agreement (MSA) with custom terms?

---

## Questions to Ask in a Vendor Demo

Beyond the checklist, use these questions to probe deeper in sales and demo conversations:

1. "Walk me through exactly what happens to our data — from input to output and beyond."
2. "What's the most common reason customers choose a competitor over you?"
3. "Show me the worst output your system has ever generated — and how you addressed it."
4. "If we need to exit the contract in 12 months, what does offboarding look like?"
5. "What do your enterprise customers wish they'd known before signing?"
